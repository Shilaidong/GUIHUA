Research Proposal

AI-Driven Enzyme Optimization and Industrial Optimization Model Design

Evolution of Glucose Dehydrogenase Based on EVOLVEpro and Its Integrated Application in Steroid Biocatalysis Systems

1. Introduction

Enzyme engineering is a cornerstone of industrial biocatalysis, enhancing catalytic efficiency, stability, and substrate specificity. Glucose dehydrogenase (GDH) is vital in biotechnological applications, particularly for cofactor regeneration in redox reactions. This study proposes leveraging AI-driven protein engineering tools to optimize GDH for advanced industrial use. Using an enhanced EVOLVEpro model, we aim to design GDH variants with superior catalytic performance. These variants will be validated through molecular docking and dynamics simulations for binding efficiency and stability, followed by synthesis and enzymatic evaluation. For industrial application, optimized GDH will be integrated into ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA) production within bile acid biosynthesis pathways. Additionally, an AI-based industrial optimization model will be developed to process outputs from biological large models, incorporating industrial parameters (e.g., pH, temperature, and production conditions) to enhance optimization reliability. This model will extend beyond UDCA synthesis, offering broader applicability in enzyme and drug molecule optimization. By integrating artificial intelligence, computational biology, and industrial bioprocess design, this research seeks to advance biocatalyst performance and contribute to industrial enzyme and pharmaceutical development.

2. Background

2.1. Research Status

Recent progress in AI-driven protein design has revolutionized enzyme engineering. Tools like EVOLVEpro and other deep learning models have optimized enzyme sequences, yielding significant performance improvements. Molecular docking and dynamics simulations provide critical insights into enzyme-substrate interactions, while experimental validation ensures functional relevance. In industrial contexts, integrating AI-optimized enzymes into large-scale bioprocesses requires computational models to predict optimal conditions. Studies on bile acid biosynthesis underscore enzyme engineering's role in cofactor regeneration, positioning GDH as an ideal target for AI-driven optimization. However, there remains a gap in enhancing industrial optimization models to effectively bridge conventional biological large models with real-world production environments‚Äîa challenge this study aims to address.

2.2. Models and Processes

2.2.1. EVOLVEpro Model

Protein engineering is essential in biology and medicine, yet traditional methods like directed evolution are labor-intensive, relying on extensive mutant libraries and high-throughput screening, often missing optimal variants. Computational approaches using protein language models (PLMs) predict functional mutations but offer limited gains. EVOLVEpro, introduced in Science (November 2024), integrates PLMs with few-shot active learning regression to streamline enzyme evolution. Using a lightweight random forest regressor, it interprets PLM embeddings and optimizes protein activity with minimal experimental data (e.g., 10 data points), outperforming zero-shot predictions across diverse proteins‚Äîe.g., enhancing antibody binding affinity (40-fold) and T7 RNA polymerase fidelity (100-fold).

In this study, I propose enhancing EVOLVEpro by replacing its random forest regressor with XGBoost, a gradient boosting decision tree model offering superior nonlinear modeling, stability, and interpretability for high-dimensional PLM outputs. An active learning mechanism combining predicted values and uncertainty estimates will further improve mutant screening efficiency within limited experimental budgets. This EVOLVEpro-XGB model is expected to elevate accuracy and sample efficiency in GDH optimization.

2.2.2. 7Œ≤-Reductase + Glucose Dehydrogenase Catalytic System and UDCA Synthesis

Ursodeoxycholic acid (UDCA), a key bile acid for hepatobiliary treatments and steroid drug intermediates, is synthesized via 7-hydroxyl epimerization of chenodeoxycholic acid (CDCA). Traditional chemical synthesis involves complex, low-yield steps with toxic reagents, posing environmental and safety risks. Early biological methods using 7Œ±- and 7Œ≤-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenases (7Œ±-HSDH and 7Œ≤-HSDH) lack cofactor regeneration, requiring costly exogenous NAD(P)H and limiting scalability.

Figure 1: 7Œ≤-Reductase + Glucose Dehydrogenase Catalytic System for UDCA Synthesis

Integrating GDH addresses these issues by regenerating NADPH via glucose oxidation, enhancing efficiency. Studies demonstrate recombinant Escherichia coli co-expressing 7Œ±-HSDH, 7Œ≤-HSDH, and GDH achieve 99% CDCA-to-UDCA conversion (100 mM, 2 hours, 2L scale), with large-scale validation at 500L. This system offers a green, efficient platform for UDCA production, which this study will further optimize.

3. Objectives and Hypotheses

This study hypothesizes that AI-driven protein engineering, centered on an optimized EVOLVEpro model, can rapidly identify high-performance GDH mutants with minimal data, improving catalytic efficiency, thermal stability, and solvent tolerance. Integrating these mutants into the 7Œ≤-HSDH-based UDCA synthesis system will enhance conversion rates and demonstrate industrial scalability and cost-effectiveness. Additionally, an AI industrial optimization model compatible with biological large models (e.g., ProtT5, AlphaFold) can bridge laboratory optimization with industrial realities by incorporating practical parameters, enhancing AI applicability.

Key Objectives:

  • Optimize EVOLVEpro Structure: Replace the random forest regressor with XGBoost to improve predictive accuracy and active learning efficiency for enzyme optimization.
  • Design and Screen GDH Mutants: Generate and select high-performance GDH variants using the enhanced EVOLVEpro model and active learning.
  • Structural and Molecular Validation: Use AutoDock and GROMACS for docking and dynamics simulations to assess mutant binding and stability.
  • Enzyme Synthesis and Testing: Express, purify, and evaluate GDH mutants' kinetics (Km, Vmax, kcat) and stability under varied conditions.
  • Industrial Integration: Integrate optimized GDH into UDCA synthesis, optimizing fermentation and validating scalability in bioreactors.
  • Develop AI Industrial Model: Construct a generalizable model integrating biological and industrial data for enzyme and drug optimization.
